Did you know?

Students may already be paying for tools they've never heard of.

Canva Design, free printing, 3D printing, LinkedIn Learning, JMP, Azure, and more are already included for many students — but a lot have never heard about them.

That means students miss out on tools that help with classes, projects, portfolios, and job applications — and some even spend extra money on things they already have through tuition. I'm running because students deserve clearer access, better awareness, and a stronger connection to the resources meant to help them succeed.

Students should not have to discover major campus resources by accident.
Technology should create opportunity, not confusion.
Better awareness can help students save money and build stronger resumes.
01 Why I'm Running

Students deserve to know what their tuition already provides.

I have met many students who do not realize they already have access to tools and services that can support their academics, creativity, and career growth. I have seen this firsthand through conversations in class and through campus technology support.

Because of that lack of awareness, students sometimes pay out of pocket for resources they already have access to. I want to help change that by pushing for better visibility, clearer communication, and easier access.

This is not just about promoting resources. It is about helping students get the full value of their tuition and use those tools to build stronger skills in a competitive job market.
